1. Introduction

Welcome to Step Alarm: Walk to Dismiss (the "App"). We are committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy.

Step Alarm is a unique alarm application that requires you to walk 10 steps to dismiss a ringing alarm. The App also offers a rewarded ad option — watch a short video to skip the walking requirement.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our App, available on the Google Play Store for Android devices.

🔒 Key Point: All alarm data is stored locally on your device only. We do not collect, transmit, or store your alarm settings on any external server. The only external data flow is through Google AdMob for displaying advertisements.

2. Data Collection

Step Alarm is an offline-first alarm application. All alarm-related data is stored locally on your device only.

3. Device Sensor Data

To provide the core step-counting feature, we access the following device sensors:

Sensor Purpose When Active Stored or Sent?
Step Counter Sensor Count total steps to dismiss alarm Only during active alarm Never
Step Detector Sensor Detect individual step events Only during active alarm Never
Accelerometer Fallback step detection when step sensors unavailable Only during active alarm Never
📱 Important: Sensor data is processed in real time, locally on your device. It is:
  • Never saved to disk
  • Never transmitted over the internet
  • Discarded immediately after the alarm is dismissed
  • Only active while an alarm is ringing (not continuous)

10. Background Processing

The App uses Android background services to operate reliably:

10.1 Foreground Services (Active Alarm Only)

Service Function When Active Notification Shown?
AlarmService Plays alarm sound & vibration Only while alarm is ringing Yes
StepCounterService Counts steps to dismiss alarm Only while alarm is ringing Yes

10.2 Alarm Scheduling (Passive)

Uses Android's AlarmManager with setAlarmClock() to trigger alarms. This is a standard Android platform API. No background process runs between alarms.

10.3 Boot Receiver

Fires once after device restart to re-schedule your saved alarms. Reads from local SharedPreferences, re-schedules via AlarmManager, then exits. No data is transmitted.

10.4 Full Screen Alarm Activity

When an alarm fires, a full-screen activity displays over the lock screen showing the real-time step counter. This is dismissed when you walk 10 steps or watch a rewarded ad.
